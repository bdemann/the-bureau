How CLEAR Works

A personal commitment tracker that takes follow-through seriously. Here's the thinking behind it.

Home › CLEAR Application › How CLEAR Works

The Problem with To-Do Lists

Most task apps work like notepads. You add something, it sits there, you either do it or you don't. The app doesn't really care which. There's no friction when you push something to tomorrow for the seventh time, no signal that a pattern is forming.

CLEAR is built on a different premise: that the gap between what you intend to do and what you actually do is information worth seeing. Not to feel bad about it — to understand it, and over time, to close it.

It's not a to-do list. It's a commitment tracker. The difference is in what happens when you don't follow through.

"A commitment you can ignore indefinitely without consequence isn't really a commitment. It's a wish." — CLEAR design notes

Core Concepts

📋
Commitment Tiers

Every item you add carries a consequence tier — from critical obligations (hard deadlines, serious fallout if missed) down to aspirational work (nice to do, no external pressure). The tier shapes how the app responds when you fall behind.

⏱
Urgency Bands

Your daily view isn't a flat list. Work sorts automatically into four bands: things due today, things recommended for today, things coming up soon, and everything else. You see the right work at the right time without manually prioritizing.

📊
The Score

A rolling accountability number, visible only to you. It rises when you follow through and falls when you miss or skip. A healthy, consistent person lands around 100–130. It's honest feedback — not a grade, not surveillance.

🔥
Streaks & Patterns

CLEAR tracks how many times in a row you've snoozed or skipped a specific item. A task snoozed once is understandable. Snoozed six times is a different conversation. The app names the pattern so you can decide what to do about it.

💬
Character Voices

Brief, contextual messages respond to what you actually do — complete a streak, skip something twice, open the app fresh in the morning. The voices vary by skin, but the underlying logic is the same: the app reacts to your behavior.

🎨
Skins

The entire vocabulary and aesthetic of the app is swappable. The default BCR theme is one option. Others use completely different terminology, characters, and color palettes — same logic, different flavor. Choose what motivates you.

How the Score Works

The Patriot Score starts at 100 and moves based on what you do. Completing work earns points. Missing, skipping, and chronically snoozing items costs points. The math is calibrated so that a person doing a reasonable amount of work consistently — not perfectly, just consistently — stays comfortably in the 100–130 range.

130 +
Patriot. Consistent, thorough follow-through. More than average. The score reflects genuine sustained effort, not just light usage.
100–129
Loyal Citizen. Solid. You're completing commitments, maintaining a streak, and staying current. This is the healthy target range for most people.
70–99
Citizen. Functional but sliding. Some misses, some snoozes. Not a crisis, but worth noticing — this is where drift usually starts.
40–69
Disengaged. A pattern of avoidance or neglect. The app is reflecting something real back at you. Worth reconsidering which commitments you've actually made.
0–39
Suspected Communist. (The BCR skin's name for rock-bottom.) Chronic misses across the board. The score is telling you something isn't working — not that you're a bad person, but that the list you've built doesn't match your life right now.

The score is private and local. It never leaves your device. No one else can see it. It exists entirely to give you honest feedback about your own follow-through.

Snoozed, Skipped, and the Snooze Ladder

CLEAR distinguishes between snoozed tasks (pushed to tomorrow — still a commitment) and skipped tasks (marked as not doing it this time — tracked, not erased). Neither is a moral failure. Both are data.

The snooze ladder tracks consecutive snoozes on a single item. At one snooze, the app notes it quietly. By six, the character voice has escalated to the highest severity tier. This isn't punishment — it's making visible a pattern that usually means one of three things: the timing is wrong, the task is too vague, or the commitment doesn't actually reflect how you want to live right now.

When a pattern like that surfaces, CLEAR gives you options: pause it (suspend without penalty — useful when life changes temporarily), convert it to an idea (preserve the aspiration without the commitment pressure), or set it to Tier IV. Tier IV is the "I want to be the kind of person who does this" tier — it tracks your aspirations with zero score consequences for skipping, snoozing, or missing. Your commitments should reflect your life, not guilt you into a version of it you haven't chosen yet.

Getting Started

  1. Install CLEAR

    Open clear.bureauofcivicresponsibility.org on your phone or desktop. From the browser menu, choose "Add to Home Screen" or "Install App" to install it as a PWA. It runs fully offline — no account required.

  2. Map your areas of responsibility

    Use the area creation wizard (the + button on the Areas of Responsibility view) to map out the areas of your life — work, health, family, finances, creative projects, whatever applies. For each area, add the routines, tasks, goals, and ideas that match your vision of what success there looks like. The app is built to help align your daily action with your actual aspirations, not to tell you what to aspire to.

  3. Check in daily

    Open the app each day. The daily view shows what matters today — sorted automatically by urgency. Complete items as you do them. Snooze honestly — not to avoid, but to defer with intention. The check-in takes as long as it takes, which will vary depending on how much is on your plate.

  4. Adjust over time

    Use the Insights view to review patterns. If something keeps getting snoozed, change the schedule, lower the tier, or convert it to an idea or Tier IV aspiration. A commitment that doesn't fit your life right now is worth understanding — maybe the timing is off, maybe the vision behind it shifted. Pause it, reclassify it, sit with it. The goal is alignment, not attrition.

  5. Choose your skin

    Open the hamburger menu (top right) and select a skin under Appearance. The BCR theme is the default — surveillance state, patriot scores, communist threat levels. If that's not your vibe, other themes offer the same functionality with completely different vocabulary and aesthetics.

Your Data

Everything CLEAR stores stays on your device in localStorage. There is no server, no account, no sync, no backup cloud. This is by design — your commitment history is yours alone. The tradeoff is that clearing your browser data or switching devices means starting fresh. Export functionality is on the roadmap.
